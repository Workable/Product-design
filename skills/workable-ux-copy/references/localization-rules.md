# Workable Localization & Style Rules

Formatting, punctuation, and regional rules for writing Workable product copy.

---

## Language Standard

**All Workable product copy is written in US English.**

When in doubt about spelling, check the **Merriam-Webster dictionary** for the preferred US spelling of a word.

Key US vs. UK spelling differences to remember:
- canceled (not cancelled)
- canceling (not cancelling)
- color (not colour)
- analyze (not analyse)
- organize (not organise)
- center (not centre)

---

## Dates

**Format:** `Month DD, YYYY`

Write the month in full. Do not abbreviate.

✅ `May 22, 2026`  
❌ `22/05/2026`, `22 May 2026`, `May 22nd, 2026`

---

## Time

**Format:** 12-hour clock with AM/PM capitalized and a space before them. Always use 4 numerals.

- Use `12:00 AM` for midnight
- Use `12:00 PM` for midday
- If communicating across multiple time zones, include the timezone code

✅ `04:00 PM`, `09:45 AM`, `11:00 AM GMT`  
❌ `4:00PM`, `09:45am`, `17:00`

Mobile apps inherit the time format specified in device settings.

---

## Numbers

- Use **numerals** (not words) for all numbers in product copy
- Use **commas** for thousands separators: `1,000`, `10,000`
- Use **full stops (periods)** for decimal separators: `1.5`, `99.9`

✅ `1,000 candidates`, `2.5 hours`  
❌ `one thousand candidates`, `two and a half hours`

---

## Currency

**Format:** `[amount] [currency code]`

Write the amount first, then the ISO currency code. Do not use currency symbols.

✅ `200 USD`, `150 EUR`, `120 GBP`  
❌ `$200`, `€150`, `£120`

---

## Percentages

- Use the `%` symbol directly after the number with no space

✅ `75%`, `100%`  
❌ `75 %`, `seventy-five percent`

---

## Oxford Comma

**Always use the Oxford comma** (the comma before "and" or "or" in a list of 3 or more items).

✅ "candidates, jobs, and reports"  
❌ "candidates, jobs and reports"

---

## Capitalization

**Sentence case throughout** — capitalize only the first word of a title, heading, label, or sentence (and proper nouns).

Do **not** use title case for UI elements (buttons, page titles, section headings, field labels).

✅ "Add team member", "Your time off balance"  
❌ "Add Team Member", "Your Time Off Balance"

**Exceptions — always capitalize:**
- Proper nouns (Workable, Workable Agent, Ask Workable, etc.)
- Named product features (Candidate Evaluator, Candidate Finder, Free Tools for Managers)
- Abbreviations and acronyms (UK, USA, UAE, ATS, HRIS)

---

## Acronyms and Abbreviations

- Write out the full term on first use, with the acronym in parentheses, if the acronym might not be known to all users
- Country abbreviations: **UK**, **USA**, **UAE** — no full stops between letters

✅ `UK`, `USA`, `UAE`  
❌ `U.K.`, `U.S.A.`, `U.A.E.`

---

## Punctuation

### Full Stops (Periods)
- Use at the end of full sentences in body text, helper text, and descriptions
- Do **not** use after button labels, field labels, page titles, or section headings
- Do **not** place a full stop inside a hyperlink — the period should come after the closing link tag

### Exclamation Marks
- Use sparingly — only in genuinely celebratory moments (e.g., a major milestone)
- Never in error messages, warnings, or alerts
- Maximum one per screen

### Ellipsis (…)
- Use for loading/transitional states: "Generating...", "Loading..."
- Don't use for trailing thoughts or to soften a statement in UI copy

### Dashes
- **Em dash (—):** Use for strong breaks in a sentence (with spaces either side in body copy)
- **En dash (–):** Use for ranges (time ranges, date ranges) — no spaces: `09:00–17:00`
- **Hyphen (-):** Use for compound words. See specific word entries in the Terminology guide.

### Brackets
- Use parentheses `()` for supplementary information within a sentence
- Use square brackets `[]` for placeholder text in templates or dynamic content

### Ampersands (&)
- Do **not** use `&` in place of "and" in UI copy — write "and" in full
- Exception: established proper names or UI that has strict space constraints

---

## Contractions

Contractions are encouraged — they make copy sound more human and conversational.

✅ "You've added a candidate", "We couldn't process your request", "Don't worry"  
❌ "You have added a candidate", "We could not process your request"

---

## Bullet Points

- Use for lists of 3 or more items
- Keep each point parallel in structure (all nouns, or all verb phrases — don't mix)
- No full stop after the last bullet if bullets are fragments; use full stops if bullets are complete sentences
- Always include a blank line before a bulleted list

---

## Regionalization

### Resume vs. CV
- Always use **"resume"** — not "CV" — even for users in regions where "CV" is standard
- See Terminology guide for full details

### British English exceptions
- The product is written in US English throughout
- Do not switch to UK English variants for UK users — consistency is more important than regional matching at this level

### Units of Measurement
Use the appropriate system based on user location:
- **US, Liberia, Myanmar** → Imperial system
- **All other countries** → Metric system

| Measurement | Metric | Imperial |
|---|---|---|
| Area | m² | sq ft |
| Distance | km | mi |
| Length | cm, m | in, ft, yd |
| Weight | g, kg | oz, lb |
| Volume | ml, L | fl oz, gal |
| Storage size | KB, GB, TB (universal) | KB, GB, TB (universal) |
| Image resolution | px, MP, ppi, dpi (universal) | px, MP, ppi, dpi (universal) |

---

## Greek Translations

When translating Workable product copy into Greek:

- Use the **singular form** of nouns where possible (Greek plurals can make UI feel overly formal or cluttered)
- Use **everyday, conversational language** — avoid formal or bureaucratic phrasing
- Maintain an **approachable tone** — the same warmth that exists in the English copy should carry through
- Do not translate product and feature names (Workable Agent, Ask Workable, etc.) — keep them in English
