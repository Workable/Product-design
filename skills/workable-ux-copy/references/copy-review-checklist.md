# Copy Review Checklist

A pre-handoff checklist for designers to self-check UI copy before passing to a UX writer
or handing to development. Run through this before any copy leaves Figma.

---

## How to use this checklist

Ask the skill: "Review this copy against the checklist" and paste your copy, or work through
it yourself. Each section covers a different failure mode.

---

## 1. Voice & Tone

- [ ] Does the copy avoid "Oops", "Whoops", "Sorry", and "Unfortunately"?
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for this moment? (Error = calm+helpful. Success = brief+warm. Onboarding = encouraging.)
- [ ] Does each piece of copy serve one clear purpose: inform, guide, or reassure?
- [ ] Have filler words been removed? (If a word doesn't add meaning, it's gone.)
- [ ] Does it sound like something a person would actually say?

---

## 2. Grammar & Perspective

- [ ] Is everything in sentence case? (Only first word + proper nouns capitalized.)
- [ ] Is it second person throughout? ("your profile", not "my profile")
- [ ] Is Workable referred to as "Workable" or "we" — not "the platform" or "the system"?
- [ ] Are contractions used where appropriate? (They make copy feel more human.)
- [ ] Is active voice used? ("You added a candidate", not "A candidate was added")

---

## 3. Buttons & CTAs

- [ ] Does every button start with a verb?
- [ ] Is the CTA 1–3 words? (Aim for 1–2.)
- [ ] Does the modal/page title verb match the primary CTA verb?
- [ ] Are all vague CTAs eliminated? (No "OK", "Confirm", "Yes", "Submit", "Click here")
- [ ] Are destructive actions clearly labeled? (Not softened — "Delete" not "Remove from list")

---

## 4. Error Messages

- [ ] Does every error message state what went wrong?
- [ ] Does it say how to fix it, or what to do next?
- [ ] Is no blame placed on the user?
- [ ] Is the word "invalid" avoided? (It's technical and cold — be specific instead.)
- [ ] Is the error message in sentence case with no exclamation marks?

---

## 5. Empty States

- [ ] Does the empty state communicate the value of the feature (not just that it's empty)?
- [ ] Is there a clear, specific action the user can take?
- [ ] Does it avoid "No data available", "Nothing here yet", and "No results"?

---

## 6. Field Labels & Forms

- [ ] Is every field label under 5 words?
- [ ] Are required fields marked with an asterisk (*)?
- [ ] Is helper text placed below the label — not inside the input as placeholder text?
- [ ] Are placeholder texts only used for format hints (e.g., "DD/MM/YYYY") — not for label substitutes?

---

## 7. Modals

- [ ] Does the modal title describe the action, not just name the object?
- [ ] Are there a maximum of 2 actions (primary + dismiss/cancel)?
- [ ] Is "Are you sure?" avoided as a title?

---

## 8. Terminology

- [ ] Is "login" used as a noun/adjective and "log in" as a verb?
- [ ] Is "setup" used as a noun/adjective and "set up" as a verb?
- [ ] Is "resume" used — not "CV"?
- [ ] Is "your" used — not "my"?
- [ ] Is "profile photo" used — not "profile picture" or "avatar"?
- [ ] Is "dropdown" one word — not "drop-down"?
- [ ] Is "view" used instead of "see" in instructions? ("View your candidates" not "See your candidates")
- [ ] Is "select" used instead of "click" for device-agnostic instructions?
- [ ] Is "self-" always hyphenated? (self-review, self-assessment)
- [ ] Is "time off" unhyphenated as a noun and hyphenated as an adjective? ("request time off" / "time-off balance")

---

## 9. AI Features

- [ ] Are only canonical AI product names used? (Workable Agent, Ask Workable, Candidate Evaluator, Candidate Finder)
- [ ] Are all forbidden terms avoided? (assistant, copilot, bot, AI Recruiter, AI-powered, generate [as generic verb])
- [ ] Do button labels start with a verb — not "Use AI" or "AI assist"?
- [ ] Do loading states use present continuous (-ing) verbs and describe the action specifically?
- [ ] Is there disclosure text anywhere the AI interaction is non-obvious or candidate-facing?
- [ ] Does the copy avoid over-promising AI results? (No "best candidate", "perfect match", "guaranteed")

---

## 10. Formatting & Localization

- [ ] Are dates in Month DD, YYYY format? (May 22, 2026 — not 22/05/2026)
- [ ] Are times in 12-hour format with AM/PM capitalized and a space before them? (09:45 AM)
- [ ] Are currencies written as "[amount] [code]"? (200 USD — not $200)
- [ ] Are numbers written as numerals — not words?
- [ ] Is the Oxford comma used in lists of 3+ items?
- [ ] Are there no full stops after button labels, headings, or field labels?
- [ ] Are links descriptive — not "click here" or "tap here"?
- [ ] Are links placed at the end of sentences (not mid-sentence)?
- [ ] Is "wifi" lowercase with no spaces or hyphens?

---

## 11. Notifications & Transactional Emails

- [ ] Is the notification one idea only?
- [ ] Does it include a link or action where relevant?
- [ ] For transactional emails: is the subject line under 60 characters and action-driven?
- [ ] Is the email CTA clearly visible and placed prominently?

---

## Quick "last look" test

Read the copy out loud. If you stumble, a user will stumble. If it sounds robotic, simplify it.
If it sounds chatty in an error message, pull it back. If you'd need to read it twice to understand
what you're supposed to do, it needs work.
